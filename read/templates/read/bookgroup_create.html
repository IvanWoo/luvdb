You are creating a Book Group.

A Book Group is an alternative way to manage book editions. For books with only one instance (most long-form books, such as novels, non-fictions, etc.), the 3-level BIBFRAME model will handle the book edition very well. But for multi-instances books, such as short story collection, we can't rely on the instances included in a book, because different editions of the same book might include different instances, or include them in different orders. In those cases, we need to group different editions with Book Group.

Examples
  • This 2007 paperback edition of The Annotated Prided and Prejudice is a reprint of the 2003 hardcover edition, both are Books of the same Instance.
  • All But Impossible is a short story collection by Edward D. Hoch, which links to 15 Instances of short stories.
